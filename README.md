COBOL_mess holds several text file that are fake COBOL programs.
The files are hand-typed from a teribally written COBOL program from a book.
"Structured COBOL Methods" by Paul Noll -- Published by Murach.

Page 117 - Chapter 6 - The 10 Most Frequently Asked Questions

Question #5 - "How do I make use of your methods when I have to maintain old programs that weren't written that way ?"

*copywrite* 1997 Mike Murach & Associates

"How to design, code, and test programs so they're easier to debug, document, and maintain."

The book is to COBOL what "Clean Code" is to Java.

The style advocated is very hierarchial.

"DRY" - Do *not* Repeat Yourself   is not attempted.
IBM mainframe products like CICS force repeated used of labels and attributes all over the place.
